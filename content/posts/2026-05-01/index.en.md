## Today at a glance

May opens loud. Simon Willison's DeepSeek V4 review is the most concrete "Chinese open model is genuinely competitive at the frontier" take we've had. Right beside it: Uber burned its full 2026 AI budget in four months, all on Claude Code. Apple accidentally shipped a CLAUDE.md file inside the Support app, confirming Anthropic's tooling has reached deep into Apple's internal engineering. Grok 4.3 shipped, Spotify added human-artist Verified badges, and TI continues its grip on US K-12 math via the TI-84 Evo. If you're tracking AI tooling commoditization, this is your day.

---

## 1. DeepSeek V4 — almost on the frontier

Tag: [EN · Simon Willison · HN Top]
Link: <https://simonwillison.net/2026/Apr/24/deepseek-v4/>

Simon's review, with hands-on benchmarks, calls V4 "almost on the frontier" — about as positive as he gets. Pair with the May 6 announcement of DeepSeek V4 Pro at 75% off through May 31: the cost-quality frontier for non-frontier models has moved meaningfully this quarter.

## 2. Uber torches its 2026 AI budget on Claude Code in four months

Tag: [EN · HN Top]
Link: <https://www.briefs.co.uk/news/uber-torches-entire-2026-ai-budget-on-claude-code-in-four-months/>

Uber's engineering org spent its full annual AI budget on Claude Code by April. The HN comment thread (475+) splits between "this is a productivity multiplier and worth every cent" and "this is what unaccountable spend on a buzzword looks like." Either way, the procurement frame is changing — AI tooling is moving from line-item to strategic capex.

## 3. Grok 4.3

Tag: [EN · HN Top]
Link: <https://docs.x.ai/developers/models/grok-4.3>

xAI keeps shipping. The docs emphasize reasoning and tool use. HN comments (530+) are mixed on actual coding-task competitiveness, but capability gap to the leaders has narrowed.

## 4. Apple accidentally left a CLAUDE.md file in the Apple Support app

Tag: [EN · HN Top]
Link: <https://x.com/aaronp613/status/2049986504617820551>

Reverse-engineering revealed an Anthropic CLAUDE.md config file inside the shipped Apple Support iOS app. Confirms Anthropic's coding tooling has reached production-side teams at Apple. Notable timing: Apple had just announced a big internal AI strategy push, and this slip lets us see what that internally actually looks like.

## 5. Show HN: WhatCable — Mac menu-bar USB-C inspector

Tag: [EN · Show HN]
Link: <https://github.com/darrylmorley/whatcable>

Reads cable capability data the OS already exposes, surfaces it as plain English. Free, open source, no tracking. Genuinely useful for the cable drawer of doom.

## 6. TI-84 Evo

Tag: [EN · HN Top]
Link: <https://education.ti.com/en/products/calculators/graphing-calculators/ti-84-evo>

TI updates the TI-84 line. HN comments (480+) split between nostalgia and frustration with TI's lock on US K-12 math testing. The lock-in itself is a remarkable case study in how regulatory and curricular dependencies can sustain a hardware franchise for decades.

## 7. Spotify adds 'Verified' badges to human artists

Tag: [EN · HN Top]
Link: <https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c5yerr4m1yno>

Spotify's response to the AI-generated music flood: a verified-human badge. The structural question — how platforms preserve provenance in an era of cheap synthetic content — applies far beyond music. Expect equivalents for video, written word, and visual art across other platforms in the next 12 months.

## 8. AI uses less water than the public thinks

Tag: [EN · HN]
Link: <https://californiawaterblog.com/2026/04/26/ai-water-use-distractions-and-lessons-for-california/>

California water experts on AI data center water use. The actual figures are an order of magnitude smaller than headline coverage suggests, especially compared to agricultural use. Useful counter-data if you're in conversations about energy/sustainability of AI workloads.

## 9. Police have used license plate readers to stalk romantic interests 14+ times

Tag: [EN · HN]
Link: <https://ij.org/police-have-reportedly-used-license-plate-readers-to-stalk-romantic-interests-at-least-14-times-in-recent-years/>

The recurring lesson: any centralized surveillance capability will eventually be misused by insiders. The 14 instances are documented; the actual count is almost certainly higher. Useful reference for any team building infrastructure that surfaces personal-movement data.

## 10. Your website is not for you

Tag: [EN · HN]
Link: <https://websmith.studio/blog/your-website-is-not-for-you/>

A short, sharp post about web design priorities. The thesis: the website serves the visitor, not the founder's aesthetic preferences. Comment thread (188) has lots of good practical examples.
